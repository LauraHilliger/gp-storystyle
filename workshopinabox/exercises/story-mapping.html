Activity

Story Mapping

After a group has been introduced to the Hero's Journey, they practice identifying story elements to describe a campaign.

Made by our pals at Dancing Fox

Steps for the Activity

  1. Facilitator hands out blank paper story maps or directs participants to Mister Fox's Story Mapping Gizmo.
  2. Decide what you're going to map. The Facilitator may show a video of a campaign ask and have everyone map it together, or give participants a choice of mapping a campaign they are familiar with, one they work on, or the overarching story of their organisation.
  3. Participants pair up and spend 20 minutes digging in to each element of the story.
  4. Final maps get posted on the wall for group inspection, question, and report back.

Discussion

Groups often have to be prompted to articulate the vision. It's not enough for a story to say what's wrong, it has to paint a picture of the world transformed. Similarly, the Monster usually wears one or more masks, and peeling back to see the real force you're fighting can be truly revealing. And remember that while in most cases, an activist organisation will be the mentor or giver of gifts, that's not a passive role: there's much that a mentor does in the field directly -- it's not just an enabling role.
